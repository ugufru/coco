# CoCo Legends

Notable software engineers, authors, and community figures from the TRS-80 Color Computer world — classic era through today.

This is a working research document. Entries are brief starting points meant to be expanded with deeper research. Details are compiled from community knowledge and should be fact-checked against primary sources (Rainbow Magazine archives, CoCo mailing list history, personal sites, CoCoFEST presentations).

---

## Game Developers

**Steve Bjork** — Spectral Associates. Zaxxon, Astro Blast, and numerous Tandy-licensed titles. One of the most prolific CoCo game developers of the 1980s.

**Nick Marentes** — Australian developer. Donut Dilemma, Gunstar, Cashman. Remarkably active into the modern era, still producing new CoCo games and writing about the development process.

**Rick Adams** — Co-creator of Dungeons of Daggorath (with Phil DeLong). Widely considered one of the greatest CoCo games ever made — a real-time 3D dungeon crawler years ahead of its time.

**Phil DeLong** — Co-creator of Dungeons of Daggorath.

**Joshua Lavinsky** — Space Warp / Time Trek (1978–80). Fit a complete real-time Star Trek simulation into 4,096 bytes of Z-80 assembly at age 16. Published by Personal Software (later VisiCorp).

**Ken Kalish** — Spectral Associates co-founder. Developed numerous CoCo game and utility titles through the 1980s.

**Steve Ostrom** — Spectral Associates. Game and utility development for the CoCo.

**Tom Mix Software** — Prolific CoCo game publisher. Doubleback, Katerpillar, and many other titles. One of the most recognizable names on CoCo game packaging.

**Robert Arnstein** — Tandy/Radio Shack in-house programmer. Wrote many of the official CoCo cassette software titles that shipped through Radio Shack stores.

**Greg Zumwalt** — CoCo game developer with multiple titles across the platform's commercial era.

**Chuck Ekblad** — Game developer, multiple CoCo titles.

**John Kowalski (SockMaster)** — Demo scene legend. Pushed CoCo graphics far beyond what anyone thought the hardware could do. His demos remain jaw-dropping technical achievements.

**Jim Gerrie** — MC-10 and CoCo BASIC game ports and originals. Prolific modern-era contributor keeping BASIC-era game development alive.

**Glen Dahlgren** — Involved with Dungeons of Daggorath preservation and revival efforts.

## OS & Systems Software

**Boisy Pitre** — NitrOS-9 lead developer. Central figure in modern CoCo OS development, keeping a real multitasking OS alive and evolving on 6809 hardware.

**L. Curtis Boyle** — NitrOS-9 Ease of Use (EOU) project lead — a pre-configured NitrOS-9 distribution that serves as a proving ground for changes before they reach the official NitrOS-9 repository. Maintains the definitive online catalog of commercial CoCo games, with screenshots, developer notes, easter eggs, and permissions-cleared downloads sourced directly from original authors. Preserved the rare 1986 Radio Shack CoCo 3 demo as downloadable disk images, conducted the Ken Kalish interview documenting Spectral Associates history, and wrote the OS9 DSK Converter utility for moving disk images to physical media. Site: lcurtisboyle.com/nitros9.

**Allen Huffman** — Sub-Etha Software (OS-9 era). CoCo blogger and community historian. Has written extensively about CoCo history and his own development experiences.

**Tim Lindner** — MAME CoCo driver work and broad community contributions to CoCo emulation accuracy.

## Tools & Compilers

**Pierre Sarrazin** — Creator of CMOC, a working C compiler targeting 6809/CoCo. A major modern cross-development tool that opened C programming to the platform.

**Ciaran Anscomb** — Author of XRoar, the primary CoCo/Dragon emulator used for cross-development. Accurate, actively maintained, and essential to the modern dev workflow.

**William Asher** — Creator of lwtools (lwasm/lwlink), the modern 6809 cross-assembler toolchain. The de facto standard assembler for contemporary CoCo development.

---

## Hardware Designers

**Ed Snider** — Creator of the CoCoSDC, an SD card interface for real CoCo hardware. Brought modern storage to vintage machines.

**Mark Marlette** — Cloud-9 Tech. CoCo-3 hardware expansions including the SuperBoard. Pushed the hardware envelope for the platform.

**Roger Taylor** — Hardware projects and community contributions spanning multiple CoCo generations.

---

## Books, Magazines & Education

**William Barden Jr.** — Wrote foundational CoCo programming books including *Color Computer Assembly Language Programming* and others. Taught a generation how the 6809 worked.

**Marty Goodman** — Deep technical articles on CoCo hardware internals. A go-to source for understanding what the machine was really doing at the signal level.

**Lonnie Falk** — Publisher of *The Rainbow Magazine*, the CoCo's essential monthly periodical. The magazine was the connective tissue of the entire CoCo community through the 1980s and into the early 1990s.

**Dale Puckett** — Rainbow Magazine contributor/editor.

---

## Community & Preservation

**John Linville** — CoCoFEST organizer and community leader. Instrumental in keeping the CoCo community connected and active through annual gatherings.

**George Phillips** — CoCo community contributor. Conducted the 2015 interview with Joshua Lavinsky that preserved important early history of TRS-80 software development.
