# SPACE WARP

The year is 2200. The United Planets system is under siege.

Jovian warships have invaded the galaxy — dozens of hostile vessels scattered across 64 sectors, closing in on UP starbases. As commander of the UP Endever, you are the last line of defense. Your orders from UP Command are simple: destroy every Jovian ship. Protect every base. There is no reinforcement coming. There is only you.

Navigate the galaxy by hyperdrive. Hunt Jovians with masers and triton missiles. Dock at starbases to repair your ion engines, deflectors, and scanners. But move fast — the Jovians aren't waiting. They drift toward your bases, respond to combat with reinforcements from adjacent sectors, and migrate toward the hottest warzones. The galaxy is alive, and it's closing in.

Every command costs energy. Every stardate ticks down your window. Push too hard and you'll find yourself adrift with dead engines in a sector full of hostiles. Play it safe and the bases fall one by one.

Originally published by Personal Software in 1980 for the TRS-80, Space Warp has been rebuilt from scratch in 6809 assembly and Forth for the TRS-80 Color Computer. New genome-driven AI gives every Jovian unique behavior, speed, and aggression. Procedurally generated sprites mean no two enemies look alike. The galaxy remembers where you fought — and sends more.

Nine difficulty levels. Semigraphics tactical combat. A living galaxy that fights back.

Good luck, Commander.
